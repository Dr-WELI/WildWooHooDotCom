Framework, rationale, and application

The creative logic behind the work

WildWooHoo’s music production follows a clear framework. We begin with nature, behavioural logic, or environmental feeling; shape that through music, imagery, rhythm, and story; and create work that connects emotionally while carrying social meaning.

Framework 01

Nature / research source

We begin with natural behaviour, animal societies, environmental change, or a research-informed line of thought.

→
Framework 02

Creative translation

Music, visual storytelling, movement, language, and artistic identity translate that source into a culturally resonant form.

→
Framework 03

Social impact

The work helps audiences recognise diversity, belonging, coexistence, and our relationship to nature through feeling first, then reflection.

An original song inspired by research on goat social hierarchy, translated into a bold, culturally grounded music concept.

Original song Natural diversity Behavioural logic
Track
Research source Presented at XLI Encontro Anual de Etologia
Framework: goat hierarchy, rank, competition, and behaviour
Creative logic: language, performance, and musical identity build the cultural frame
Impact: behavioural dynamics become socially recognisable and affectively engaging

Linked to research on social hierarchy in goats, this project bridges animal and human worlds through interconnected narratives: a scientific narrative about rank, competition, and behaviour is aligned with a cultural narrative shaped through language, performance, and musical identity.

Rather than illustrating science literally, the song transforms behavioural dynamics into a socially recognisable cultural expression, allowing audiences to engage affectively with ideas of hierarchy, conflict, and individuality.

Benevolence

A 3-song EP and trilogy music video currently in development, designed as a contemporary pop music release that carries science-inspired ideas into formats audiences would normally consume as mainstream music.

EP + trilogy video Changing environments In development
Framework: change, choice, memory, and environmental feeling
Creative logic: contemporary pop release carrying subtle science communication
Impact: expands the studio’s second area of impact through a joyful message for social change

Benevolence explores change and choice, touching on environmental change and on how humans perceive chance, choice, and memory around critical historical events.

Similar to Kangaroo Time, the concept bridges science and culture through interconnected narratives: a scientific or research-informed line of thought is woven together with cultural and emotional storytelling, allowing the work to function both as pop music and as a subtle entry point to broader science communication.

The project carries a joyful message for social change while expanding the studio’s second area of impact.

Why this work matters

Track record and proof

  • Kangaroo Time Dr WELI’s award-winning project reinterpreted his PhD research on kangaroo behaviour through music and dance.
  • Public reach The project garnered over 145,000 views, media coverage in 250+ outlets, and enthusiastic responses from schools, researchers, critics, and the public.
  • Studio insight It revealed a cultural gap: people connect differently when knowledge is carried through emotionally and culturally meaningful forms.
Impact focus

How this connects nature and social impact

  • Natural diversity and our relationship with one another We explore how individual differences, group life, cooperation, and social behaviour shape the way we relate to each other, often using animal societies as a mirror for human life.
  • Changing environments and our belonging to nature We create work that reconnects people with the understanding we are part of the animal kingdom and part of nature, especially in times of environmental change, uncertainty, and disconnection.
