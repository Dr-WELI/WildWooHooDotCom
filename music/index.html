Why this work matters

When environmental knowledge becomes culturally resonant, people respond affectively

Dr WELI has spent over a decade in Australia as a researcher, educator, and creative. His award-winning project Kangaroo Time reinterpreted PhD research on kangaroo behaviour through music and dance.

The project garnered over 145,000 views, media coverage in 250+ outlets, and strong responses from schools, researchers, critics, and the public. It revealed a cultural gap: people engage differently when knowledge is translated into emotionally and culturally resonant forms.
