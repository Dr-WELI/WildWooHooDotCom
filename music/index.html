Track

Kangaroo Time

An original song translating kangaroo behavioural research into a culturally resonant music world, connecting science, movement, and public feeling.

Listen to track
Artistic rationale

Linked to Dr WELI’s PhD research on kangaroo behaviour, this project bridges animal and human worlds through interconnected narratives: a scientific narrative about social behaviour is aligned with a cultural narrative expressed through music, dance, and identity.

The work reframes kangaroo social life in a human-facing artistic language, allowing audiences to recognise diversity, individuality, and coexistence through a more emotional and culturally familiar format.

PhD thesis Behavioural Ecology publication
Impact focus: Natural diversity

Kangaroo Time (Club Edit)

A club-oriented reinterpretation of the Kangaroo Time universe, extending the project into a more dance-driven sonic space while preserving its conceptual roots.

Listen to release
Artistic rationale

This version carries the same research lineage as Kangaroo Time, but shifts the cultural narrative toward collective movement, nightlife, and embodied participation. It bridges kangaroo and human social worlds through rhythm and dance-floor energy.

The scientific story remains connected to social behaviour and individuality, while the artistic treatment opens the work to broader public engagement through club culture.

Track

Cabra da Peste (G.O.A.T.)

An original song inspired by research on goat social hierarchy, translated into a bold, culturally grounded music concept.

Listen to track
Artistic rationale

Linked to research on social hierarchy in goats, this project bridges animal and human worlds through interconnected narratives: a scientific narrative about rank, competition, and behaviour is aligned with a cultural narrative shaped through language, performance, and musical identity.

Rather than illustrating science literally, the song transforms behavioural dynamics into a socially recognisable cultural expression, allowing audiences to engage affectively with ideas of hierarchy, conflict, and individuality.

Research source Presented at XLI Encontro Anual de Etologia
Impact focus: Natural diversity

Why this work matters

When environmental knowledge is translated into culturally resonant forms, people respond affectively

Dr WELI has spent over a decade in Australia as a researcher, educator, and creative. His award-winning project Kangaroo Time reinterpreted his PhD research on kangaroo behaviour through music and dance.

The project garnered over 145,000 views, media coverage in 250+ outlets, and enthusiastic responses from schools, researchers, critics, and the public. It revealed a cultural gap: people connect differently when knowledge is carried through emotionally and culturally meaningful forms.
